Stumped?

The Sheffield Botanical Gardens feature a fossil stump of ancient lycopod Lepidodendron, one of the giant club mosses which flourished in the Carboniferous period.

Approximately how old is this stump?

A: 100 million years old
B: 200 million years old
C: 300 million years old
D: 400 million years old

C: 300 million years old

When was Weston?

Weston Park Museum was previously a private house known as Weston Hall, and the park was its extensive estate.

In which year was Weston Park Museum opened to the public?

A: 1855
B: 1875
C: 1895
D: 1905

Atrium of the Heart

The Engineering Heartspace project is the creation of a quadruple height atrium between two buildings, housing new laboratories, offices, social spaces and the complete refurbishment of both buildings.
Between which two buildings does this atrium lie between?

A: Alfred Denny and Ella Armitage buildings
B: Sir Frederick Mappin and Central Wing buildings
C: The Diamond and Arts Tower
D: Firth Court and Information Commons

B: Sir Frederick Mappin and Central Wing buildings
